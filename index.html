About factory

The Malyshev Factory

Is a state-owned manufacturer of heavy equipment in Kharkiv, Ukraine. It was named after the Soviet politician Vyacheslav Malyshev. The factory is part of the state concern, Ukroboronprom.

It produces diesel engines, farm machinery, coal mining, sugar refining, and wind farm equipment, but is best known for its production of Soviet tanks, including the BT tank series of fast tanks, the famous T-34 of the Second World War, the Cold War T-64 and T-80, and their modern Ukrainian successor, the T-84.

The factory is closely associated with the Morozov Design Bureau (KMDB), designer of military armoured fighting vehicles and the Kharkov Engine Design Bureau for engines. In 1958, it developed the Kharkovchanka, an off-road vehicle which reached the South Pole the following year.

Our location

The location of Kharkiv's Malyshev Factory is situated in the city of Kharkiv, Ukraine. Kharkiv, the second-largest city in Ukraine, holds a significant industrial heritage, and the Malyshev Factory, named after the Soviet politician Vyacheslav Malyshev, is an integral part of this industrial landscape.

The factory's location in Kharkiv, a major industrial and cultural hub in Eastern Ukraine, contributes to its historical significance and economic impact on the region.

With its roots dating back to the late 19th century, the Malyshev Factory has played a crucial role in the development of heavy machinery, armored vehicles, and other industrial products, shaping both the local and national economy.
